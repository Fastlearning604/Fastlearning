    CONSIDERING

    The legal framework on learning in Colombia is made up of a series of laws, decrees and policies that establish the principles, rights and responsibilities related to education and learning. Below is a detailed summary of the main components of the legal framework that regulate learning: ### 1. **Political Constitution of Colombia** **Article 67** - **Description**: Establishes that education It is a fundamental right and a public service with a social function.The State is responsible for regulating and monitoring education to ensure its quality and comply with its fines. This article highlights the importance of access to education, the comprehensive training of students and the role of the State in guaranteeing these objectives. ### 2. **Law 115 of 1994 (General Education Law)** **Article 4** - **Description**: Define the responsibilities of the State, society and the family in quality and access to education. Establishes that the Nation and territorial entities must guarantee educational coverage, the qualification of educators, and promote innovation and educational research. **Article 42** - **Description**: Regulates the creation and operation of non-formal education programs and establishments (now called education for work and human development), and the issuance of occupational aptitude certificates.Establishes that these areas are subject to the regulations of the National Government. ### 3. **Law 1064 of 2006** **Article 1** - **Description**: Replaces the name of non-formal education with education for work and human development, and recognizes it as an integral part of the public educational service. This law focuses on training in work and technical skills. **Article 2** - **Description**: Recognizes education for work and human development as an essential component of the educational process and the public educational system. **Article 3**
